About me

I am a theoretical physicist and Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA) Postdoctoral Fellow at the Physik-Institut, University of Zurich, working in the group of Prof. Dr. Tomaš Bzdušek.

My research revolves around topological phases of matter on unconventional lattice geometries. I am particularly interested in Cayley lattices — a class of crystalline structures built from group-theoretic constructions (Cayley graphs) that can naturally host non-Abelian gauge fields — and in hyperbolic lattices, the discrete analogs of periodic structures in negatively curved space. A major goal is to bridge these abstract mathematical structures with experiments, especially through topolectric circuits (LC circuit networks that realize topological band structures).

Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Konstanz (with Dr. Marco Marciani) and at the University of Würzburg (with Prof. Dr. Ronny Thomale and Prof. Dr. Igor Boettcher). I completed my PhD at École Normale Supérieure de Lyon under the supervision of Dr. Pierre Delplace, where I studied Floquet topological phases in photonic systems.
